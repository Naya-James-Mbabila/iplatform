Eye Health Guide

Conjunctival Redness

May indicate allergies, infections, or systemic inflammation.

  • Allergic conjunctivitis - Itchy, watery eyes, often seasonal
  • Dry eye syndrome - Burning sensation, foreign body feeling
  • Uveitis - Pain, light sensitivity, potential vision loss
When to see a doctor: If redness persists >48 hours, is painful, or affects vision
Scleral Yellowing

Can signal liver dysfunction or hemolytic conditions.

  • Jaundice - Bilirubin buildup from liver disease
  • Gilbert syndrome - Mild, inherited condition
  • Liver cirrhosis - Advanced scarring of the liver
Urgent: New onset yellowing requires prompt medical evaluation
Prevention Tips
UV Protection

Wear sunglasses with 100% UV protection to prevent cataracts and macular degeneration.

Screen Time

Follow the 20-20-20 rule: every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds.

Nutrition

Foods rich in lutein (leafy greens) and omega-3s (fish) support eye health.
